myths you shouldn't believe


Carbs are bad!

Choose whole grains like brown rice, wheat, Baja and rage. These are rich in water-soluble vitamins, minerals, and fibre, and can help regular your blood sugar levels.

Do not eat fruits

Just because fruits are sweet, doesn't mean a diabetic can't have them every day. Fruits are a good source of antioxidant-rich vitamins, minerals, and contain high levels of fibre.

You can't eat sweets

If you have diabetes this doesn't mean that you have to exclude all sweet treats from your diet, but keep them to every now and then rather than every day.

Plate Method

The Diabetes Plate Method is the easiest way to create healthy meals that can help manage blood sugar. Using this method, you can create perfectly portioned meals with a healthy balance of vegetables, protein, and carbohydrates —without any counting,calculating, weighing, or measuring.

  • 50% nonstarchy vegetables
  • 25% lean protein, such as lentils, tofu, fish, or skinless chicken or turkey
  • 25% high fiber carbohydrates, such as whole grains or legumes
